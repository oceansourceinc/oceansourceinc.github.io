Artisan Kelp Treats

From seaweed burgers to sweet & spicy kelp pickles, Ocean Source is passionate about creating healthy, sustainable foods. We primarly focus on kelp due to its incredible lifecycle (some species can grow up to two feet a day!) and amazing health benefits. Some of our products are staples in our lineup, while others are limited-edition as we experiment with what the ocean gives us during our foraging.

Why Kelp?

Kelp offers several benefits to the individual and the planet.

1. Kelp is unbelievably sustainable. It can be harvested so that it grows right back, or, since it's an annual growth, taken after it reproduces with zero harm to the future population. It also grows faster than bamboo, with some species able to grow 2 feet per day.

2. Kelp is an antioxidant-rich superfood. It's both full of nutrients and low in fat, containing tons of minerals, vitamin K, calcium, iron, and it's even been shown to have cancer-fighting capabilities.

3. Kelp is fantastic for the environment. Coastal ecosystems can sequester up to 20 times more carbon than terrestrial forests, and do it more efficiently—unlike trees and other plants that are likely to have their sequestered carbon released back into the atmosphere after a short time, kelp often floats stored carbon out to sea, where it sinks and is sequestered away from the atmosphere for much longer (possibly millions of years!).

Mussels & Kelp Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 2 Cups Broth or Water
  • 1/2 Pound Dried Noodles
  • 1/2 Scallion, chopped
  • 1 Egg
  • 2 Tbsp Chili Flakes
  • 1 Tbsp Hot Sesame Oil
  • 1/2 Tbsp Garlic
  • 16 Large Mussels
  • 15g Sea Lettuce, dried
  • 100g Laminaria, fresh
  • 300g Feather Boa Kelp, fresh
  • 30g Nori, dried

Directions:

  1. Steam mussles for 15 min. and set aside
  2. Cut laminaria into as thin of strips as possible, like thin noodles
  3. Bring broth or water to a boil
  4. Boil laminaria, feather boa kelp & noodles until noodles are cooked
  5. Remove feather boa kelp and set aside
  6. Beat egg and add to boiling broth
  7. Turn down heat and add chili flakes, sesame oil, scallions, and garlic
  8. Cut sea lettuce into 1-inch squares
  9. Crumble nori into flakes
  10. Remove 12 mussles from shells and add to broth
  11. Add nori and sea lettuce
  12. Dish out and add 2 whole mussles (including shells) to bowls
  13. Enjoy!
